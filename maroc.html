Five times a day, a voice rises above the din of Djemaa El Fna Square as the muezzin calls the faithful from the Koutoubia Mosque to prayer.

The mosque's minaret has watched over the old city since the Almohads built it in the 12th century. Century. Today it is the most famous monument in Marrakech. The tower is a monumental cheat sheet of Moorish ornamentation: scalloped keystone arches, jagged battlements and mathematically pleasing proportions.

Imitation is said to be the greatest compliment, and the 250-foot-tall 12th-century minaret has a reputation as an architectural muse. It is the prototype of the Giralda in Seville, Spain and Le Tour Hassan in Rabat, the capital of Morocco. Unlike mosques in the Middle East, which have domed minarets, the koutoubia's square design is a hallmark of Amazigh. Inside the minaret there are no stairs, just a ramp that the muezzin would have climbed on horseback to call to prayer.

The minaret of the Koutoubia Mosque is topped with a spire of brass balls. According to local legend, the orbs were once made of gold and were "gifted" to the mosque by the wife of Almohad Sultan Yacoub Al Mansour, who melted down her jewels as punishment after she was seen eating during the fasting period of Ramadan.
